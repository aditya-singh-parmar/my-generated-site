Ucluelet</span></h4><div class="accordion-content"><h5>Getting to Salmon Eye Charters in Ucluelet BC</h5> <p><b>Getting to Ucluelet is easier than you might think! </b></p> <p>Along with some of the best salmon fishing in the world, we are easy to get to and have less boat traffic than many “remote” fly-in resorts. Unlike some of the “remote” resorts, that may have five other lodges within a short distance and up to a hundred boats fishing the same small area, in Ucluelet you can enjoy small town hospitality and uncrowded fishing grounds.</p> <div class="accordion-content-divider"></div> <h5>Coming from Canada</h5> <p><b>Getting to Ucluelet by Air</b></p> <p>There are three airports on Vancouver Island within a scenic 3 hour drive* to Ucluelet. Rental cars are available at all airports listed below.</p> <ul> <li><a href="#">Comox</a> 208 km (112 mi) 2.75 hours driving</li> <li><a href="#">Nanaimo</a> 180 km (112 mi) 2.5 hours driving</li> <li><a href="#">Tofino</a> 26 km (16 mi) 30 minutes driving</li> </ul> <p><i>*driving times are approximate</i></p> <p style="margin-top: 20px"><b>TIPS</b></p> <ul> <li>For our Alberta guests, <a href="#">WestJet</a> offers direct flights from Calgary and Edmonton to Comox, BC.</li> <li>East Coast guests may want to fly into <a href="#">Vancouver International Airport</a> before connecting to one of the airports listed above.</li> <li>Commuter flights from Vancouver to Tofino (the closest airport to Ucluelet) are available through <a href="#">KD Air</a>.</li> </ul> <div class="accordion-content-divider"></div> <h5>Coming from the USA</h5> <p><b>Getting to Ucluelet by Air</b></p> <p>You have a few options.</p> <p><b>Fly to Vancouver Island</b> – Flights to Vancouver Island are sometimes not much more expensive, and can save you driving time, compared with flights to the mainland (Vancouver). Regardless of your choice, you’ll enjoy a beautiful drive to Ucluelet*. The largest Vancouver Island airports are:</p> <ul> <li><a href="#">Victoria</a> 288 km (179 mi) 4 hours driving</li> <li><a href="#">Comox</a> 208 km (112 mi) 2.75 hours driving</li> <li><a href="#">Nanaimo</a> 180 km (112 mi) 2.5 hours driving</li> </ul> <p><i>*driving times are approximate</i></p> <p style="margin-top: 20px"><b>TIPS</b></p> <ul> <li>United Airlines offers direct flights from San Francisco to Victoria, BC.</li> </ul> <p><img src="https://billwebstudio.com/projects/Pasha/fishing/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/boat.jpg" alt=""></p> <p><b>Fly to Vancouver</b> – Most of our US guests fly into <a href="#">Vancouver International Airport</a></p> <p style="margin: 20px 15px">From there you have three choices.</p> <p><b>1. Rent a car and travel to Vancouver Island via BC Ferries </b></p> <p>BC Ferries offers service from the mainland (Vancouver) to Vancouver Island at two points: the Horseshoe Bay Terminal in North Vancouver and the Tsawwassen Terminal, south of Vancouver. Ferry service from Horseshoe Bay arrives at Departure Bay Nanaimo, whereas service from Tsawwassen arrives in Swartz Bay near Victoria, or at Duke Point, south Nanaimo. Nanaimo is closest to Ucluelet.</p> <p>You can find <a href="#">detailed driving directions to BC Ferry terminals</a> on their website. For more information or to make a reservation, visit BC Ferries:<br> <a href="#">www.bcferries.com</a>, 1-888-769-3766.</p> <p style="margin-top: 20px"><b>2. Arrange a connecting flight to Vancouver Island</b></p> <p>*See the Vancouver Island airports listed above. All airports have car rentals available.</p> <p style="margin-top: 20px"><b>3. Take a commuter flight to Tofino</b></p> <p>This is the closest you can fly to Ucluelet. KD Air offers flights directly from Vancouver to Tofino, and Ucluelet is a short drive away (aprox. 30 min.).</p> <div class="accordion-content-divider"></div> <h5>Driving to Ucluelet</h5> <p>From the #1 Hwy in Vancouver – take the Horseshoe Bay ferry out of North Vancouver. This will take you to Departure Bay, in Nanaimo.</p> <p><b>Driving directions from Departure Bay, Nanaimo to Ucluelet:</b></p> <p> 1) Take highway 19 and travel north for 43 km (26 miles)<br> 2) Take Exit 60 onto Highway 4 also known as the Pacific Rim Highway (traveling west) for 156 km (97 miles). <br> 3) At Port Alberni continue going straight down the hill, and turn right at the T-junction. From there, follow Pacific Rim Highway. <br> 4) At the T-junction for Ucluelet and Tofino, turn left to Ucluelet (aprox. 7 minutes to town) </p> <p style="margin: 20px 15px">From the US you can travel through Port Angeles and take the ferry to Victoria, or you can drive across the USA/Canada border to Tsawwassen (South of Vancouver) and take the ferry to Duke Point (south of Nanaimo).</p> <p><b>Driving directions from Duke Point, Nanaimo to Ucluelet:</b></p> <p> 1) Take highway 19 and travel north for 43 km (26 miles) <br> 2) Take Exit 60 onto Highway 4 also known as the Pacific Rim Highway (traveling west) for 156 km (97 miles). <br> 3) At Port Alberni continue going straight down the hill, and turn right at the T-junction. From there, follow Pacific Rim Highway. <br> 4) At the T-junction for Ucluelet and Tofino, (aprox. 7 minutes to Ucluelet and 25 mins to Tofino)</p> <p style="margin: 20px 15px">Here are some approx driving times to Tofino or Ucluelet including the ferry ride, border wait, and waiting for the ferries. During July and August the border wait can be around 30-45 minutes. If you drive through Port Angeles the border waits are much less.</p> <ul> <li>from Vancouver 5.5 hours</li> <li>from Seattle 7 hours</li> <li>from Portland 9 hours</li> </ul> <p><b>Directions to Boat Basin (Ucluelet, B.C.)</b></p> <p>Head north from Nanaimo Ferries, Naniamo Airport, Victoria Airport or south from Comox Airport. Once you get to Qualicum, exit and take Hwy 4 to Port Alberni. Go through Port Alberni and take a right at the T intersection.</p> <p style="margin-bottom: 20px">After about an hour you will come to another T intersection. You can go right to Tofino or left to Ucluelet.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></section><section id="content-section-5"><div class="gdlr-full-size-wrapper gdlr-show-all" style="padding-bottom: 0px; background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p><a class="btn-find-out" href="/contact-us/">Book Now</a></p> </div><div class="clear"></div><div class="clear"></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-6"><div class="gdlr-full-size-wrapper gdlr-show-all" style="padding-top: 40px; padding-bottom: 20px; background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="one-fifth column"></div><div class="one-fifth column"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p class="m0 centered"><a class="btn-btm" href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/guided-fishing-trips/victoria/">Fishing in Victoria BC <i class="gdlr-icon fa icon-chevron-right" style="color: #ffffff; font-size: 12px; "></i></a></p> </div></div><div class="one-fifth column"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p class="m0 centered"><a class="btn-btm" href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/guided-fishing-trips/">Guided Fishing Trips <i class="gdlr-icon fa icon-chevron-right" style="color: #ffffff; font-size: 12px; "></i></a></p> </div></div><div class="one-fifth column"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p class="m0 centered"><a class="btn-btm" href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/guided-fishing-trips/haida-gwaii/">Haida Gwaii Fishing <i class="gdlr-icon fa icon-chevron-right" style="color: #ffffff; font-size: 12px; "></i></a></p> </div></div><div class="one-fifth column"></div><div class="clear"></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section></div> <!-- Below Sidebar Section--> </div>
