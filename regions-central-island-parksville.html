Often featured as one of the premier locations to be visited in Vancouver Island for young families, Parkville’s natural, laid back culture, along with its easy accessibility and central locations will make you want to come back again and again.<br> Parksville enjoys one of the finest climates in all Canada. There are six provincial parks located within thirty minutes drive for easy and moderate walking. A popular destination spot for tourists during the summer, Parksville’s spectacular views of the Strait of Georgia and the Vancouver Island Mountain Range, capped with snow even on a hot summer beach day, only adds to this quiet communities charm.</p> </div><div class="clear"></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-3"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper " style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="six columns"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#969df7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4 style="text-align: left;">Brant Wildlife Festival</h4> <p>If you happen to come to Parksville in April, you might think that Alfred Hitchcock’s “The Birds” has become a reality. The annual&nbsp;Brant Wildlife Festival&nbsp;celebrates the migration of nearly 20,000 Black Brant geese from California and Mexico to their summer breeding grounds in Alaska. The beaches between Qualicum and Parksville host the annual migration of thousands of Brant Geese.</p> </div></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#e8e8e8;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5; color: #828994; "><h4>The Parksville Sand Castles:</h4> <p>The Parksville Beach Festival, held in August, hosts the Canadian Open Sand Sculpting Competition. Tens of thousands of visitors are attracted annually to this event. The creations are made during early August, but they still remain standing until the end of the month.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="six columns"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#e8e8e8;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5; color: #828994; "><h4>Parksville Beach</h4> <p>Parksville summers can be described in three words: sun, swimwear and beaches. It is an excellent location to spot and spend time relaxing on the exposed shorelines. The sand here is like cement, and the long tides makes it perfect for making sand castles.</p> </div></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#9ea4f7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>Rhododendron Lake</h4> <p>Some of the most beautiful and dynamic natural colors on the island can be found on each spring, late May or early June. Pink rhododendrons, growing wild on the shoreline of this beautiful lake are a spectacular sight. Botanists believe these species belong to a strain that survived the Ice Age. Access to Rhododendron Lake and the 5-acre groove is by private logging road. You can find signs posted at the entrance to Northwest Bay Logging Division, located approximately 7.2 km south of Parksville.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-5"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper " style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><h3 style="text-align: center;">Outdoor Activities</h3> </div><div class="clear"></div><div class="six columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-circle"><div class="box-with-circle-icon" style="background-color: #91d549"><i class="fa fa-bookmark" style="color:#ffffff;"></i><br></div><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4>Kayaking</h4> <p>The mild conditions and the vast coastline makes Parksville a perfect place for kayakers just starting out. If you don’t have the gear, local outfitters will provide you with everything you need, lessons included. A good place to launch and set out on a treek is the federal dock at French Creek.</p> </div></div></div><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-circle"><div class="box-with-circle-icon" style="background-color: #dd3333"><i class="fa fa-bookmark" style="color:#ffffff;"></i><br></div><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4>Hammerfest</h4> <p>Adventurous mountain bikers will enjoy the challenging trails located at Hammerfest Race Course, which mixes fast logging road with narrow, technical single-track 6.7 miles. An easier challenge can be found at Top Bridge Mountain Bike Park on the Englishman River, where moderate adventuring awaits. To reach the park, turn west off Highway 14, weight scales at Kaye Road, then turn to Chattell Road and follow its end.</p> </div></div></div><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-circle"><div class="box-with-circle-icon" style="background-color: #1e73be"><i class="fa fa-bookmark" style="color:#ffffff;"></i><br></div><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4>Fishing</h4> <p>Several angling options are available around Parksville, depending upon the season. &nbsp;Salmon are caught in June and July is the waters off French Creek, 5 km north of Parksville on Hwy 19A. The annual Coho salmon run up in front of French Creek &nbsp;Harbour along the beach in October. French Creek Marina has a &nbsp;public boat launch adjacent to the federal dock and Lasqueti Island Ferry. For more on Vancouver Island salmon <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/things-to-do/fishing/">fishing</a>…</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="six columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-circle"><div class="box-with-circle-icon" style="background-color: #dd3333"><i class="fa fa-bookmark" style="color:#ffffff;"></i><br></div><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4>Short Hikes</h4> <p><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/short-hikes-by-region/central-island/little-qualicum-falls/">Little Qualicum Falls&nbsp;</a>&nbsp;Provincial Park and <em><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/short-hikes-by-region/central-island/englishman-river-falls/">Englishman River Falls</a></em>&nbsp; provide some meandering trails that will speak to the heart of anyone who enjoys the sound and the feel of nature. A mere walk in the park is needed to enjoy the spectacular views.&nbsp; Mount Arrowsmith Trail, ascending the lower slopes of Mount Arrowsmith then keeps ascending all the way up to the 1.829-metre summit of <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/medium-hikes-by-region/central-island/arrowsmith/"><em>Mt. Arrowsmith</em>&nbsp;</a>&nbsp; is a good location for those who want a little more adventure. At a leisurely pace, the hike is a long day long, a two day overnighter.</p> </div></div></div><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-circle"><div class="box-with-circle-icon" style="background-color: #1e73be"><i class="fa fa-bookmark" style="color:#ffffff;"></i><br></div><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4>Skatepark</h4> <p>Waterfront skatepark is an ideal location for skateboarders, BMX riders, and in-line skaters. This park is regarded as one of the best skate parks on Vancouver Island.</p> </div></div></div><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-circle"><div class="box-with-circle-icon" style="background-color: #91d549"><i class="fa fa-bookmark" style="color:#ffffff;"></i><br></div><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4>Marinas</h4> <p>Public boat launches are provided at French Creek Marina, located on the Island Highway between Qualicum Beach and Parksville. Marinas with moorage are available in Beachcomber Marina, Nanoose Bay and Schooner Cove. All three are friendly and welcome visitors, offering full services nearby, restaurants included.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-6"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper gdlr-show-all no-skin" style="background-color: #efefef; "><div class="container"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><h3 style="text-align: center;">Provincial Parks &amp; Caving</h3> </div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#9ea4f7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>Rathtrevor Beach Provincial Park:</h4> <p><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/beach-icon.png" rel="attachment wp-att-10519"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-10519" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083942/vancouver-island/beach-icon.png" alt="beach icon" width="63" height="33"></a>The biggest and one of the most popular beaches in the area, offers great swimming conditions, depending upon the season. This beach is one of the vastest expanses of beach sand in all BC, the beach stretching out nearly a kilometer out of the sea when the tide is low. Strait of Georgia waters tend to warm up quickly during summer, as the tide rises over the hot sand making it one of the best swimming locations on the entire island. Plenty of campsites can be found in the park, but they fill up quickly, so calling ahead of time is recommended. The beach gets its name from the Rath family, who once owned the land where the park resides.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#a3a8f7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>Little Qualicum Falls Provincial Park:</h4> <p><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/park2-icon.png" rel="attachment wp-att-10523"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-10523" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083942/vancouver-island/park2-icon.png" alt="park2 icon" width="63" height="33"></a>This park is one of highlights of the area is worth exploring in any season. The trails go along the Little Qualicum River and in the warmer months you can bring your swimming gear to enjoy the crystal clear water.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#949bf7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>Cedar Grove</h4> <p><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/cove-icon.png" rel="attachment wp-att-10520"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-10520" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083942/vancouver-island/cove-icon.png" alt="cove icon" width="63" height="33"></a>MacMillan Provincial Park is home to the infamous stand of ancient and resilient Douglas Fir known as <em><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/short-hikes-by-region/central-island/cathedral-grove/">Cathedral Grove</a></em>. Its hundreds of feet tall canopy shelter visitors, leaving them amazed at the magnificence of these Vancouver Island marvels. Some of the Douglas-fir here are 800 years old, attracting and captivating visitors leaving them humbled by their natural enormity. Trails can be found on either side of the highway. The northern trail goes through groves of ancient Western Red Cedar toward the shores of Cameron Lake while the south loop showcases the largest Douglas-fir trees, biggest one measuring over 9 meters in circumference. MacMillan can be found on Highway 4 on the shores of Cameron Lake, 17 miles west of Qualicum Beach.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#9ea4f7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>Horne Lake Caves Provincial Park- Caves</h4> <p><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/park1-icon.png" rel="attachment wp-att-10522"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-10522" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083943/vancouver-island/park1-icon.png" alt="park1 icon" width="63" height="33"></a>Anyone into spelunking? Squeezing, bending, crawling through this unique cave network is not an opportunity to be missed. Seven caves in the Horne Lake Cave system are protected by the park. Tours are available from July through August. There is a small fee, but the friendly and knowledgeable guides from Canadian Cave Conservancy will help you have an unforgettable adventure. As an alternative, self-guided tours are available to Main Cave and Lower Main Cave throughout the year.&nbsp; Tip: Grab your boots, warm clothes flashlights and headlamps. Adrenaline junkies should t ry the Riverbed Bottoming trip. This is a 3-4 hour guided tour that leads you through a series of vertical pits, deepest one being nearly 19 meters.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#9ba1f7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>&gt;Kulth Music Festival</h4> <p><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/festival-icon.png" rel="attachment wp-att-10521"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-10521" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083943/vancouver-island/festival-icon.png" alt="festival icon" width="63" height="33"> </a>Every mid-July, the Kulth Music Fest is a unique venue held in many communities near Qualicum and Parksville. The festival was born as a method to celebrate and protect the natural environment through music and entertainment. It is a great experience for people of all age—artists, international and local are invited performing music in their preferred styles</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-7"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper " style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><h3 style="text-align: center;">History</h3> <p>The first Europeans to discover and explore this part of Vancouver Island were the Spanish voyagers in 1791.
