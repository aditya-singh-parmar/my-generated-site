                      CENTRAL ISLANDS

It is known to be a place to take young families since the warm weather, beaches, short walks and slow pace make it an ideal vacation spot. Parksville and Qualicum Beach have many resorts for those wanting to stay right on the water. There are small communities around that have events going on year round. </div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-title-item"><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Top things to do:</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083928/vancouver-island/little-qualicum2-scenic.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Little_Qualicum2-Scenic.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Scenic walks</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p>There are many easy short scenic walks (we call them short hikes) within a few minutes of <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/regions/central-island/parksville/">Parksville</a> or <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/top-10-beaches-on-vancouver-island/honorable-mentions/qualicum-beach/">Qualicum</a></em> to <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/short-hikes-by-region/central-island/cathedral-grove/">Cathedral Grove</a>, Little <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/short-hikes-by-region/central-island/little-qualicum-falls/">QualicumFalls</a>,and <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/short-hikes-by-region/central-island/englishman-river-falls/">Englishman River Falls</a></em>. The <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/regions/central-island/nanaimo/">Nanaimo Harbour</a> <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/regions/central-island/nanaimo/">walk</a></em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/regions/central-island/nanaimo/">or</a> the trails through <em>Beban Park</em>are beautiful walks in downtown <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/regions/central-island/nanaimo/">Nanaimo</a>. The Deep Bay Spit is a great short drive during spring months to watch birds in with a scenic background.</p> </div></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083930/vancouver-island/chinoobigqualicumriver-700.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/ChinooBigQualicumRiver-700.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Rivers</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item" style="margin-bottom: 60px;"><p>The Big Qualicum fish hatchery has trout in a rearing pond during summer months. The Chinook salmon start coming up in late August and can be seen in holding pens until late September. Coho salmon can be seen during October here and Chum salmon during November. You are allowed to fly fish the Big Qualicum, Little Qualicum, Englishman and Nanaimo Rivers for returning salmon as well as trout during summer and fall seasons. Check with DFO for regulations.</p> </div></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083930/vancouver-island/hockey.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Hockey.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Junior Hockey</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item" style="margin-bottom: 50px;"><p>There is Junior AA Hockey with the Nanaimo Clippers in Nanaimo and the Alberni Valley Bulldogs in Port Alberni from September to March. The Junior B Oceanside Generals play in Parksville and this league is often a little tougher with more physical play. All three hockey teams are entertaining.</p> </div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-3"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper gdlr-show-all no-skin" style="background-color: #f2f2f2; padding-top: 90px; padding-bottom: 60px; "><div class="container"><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083931/vancouver-island/nile-creek-fishing.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Nile-creek-fishing.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Nile Creek fly fishing</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p>A private group led by Ken Kirkby decided to restore Nile creek to its original glory with Cutthroat trout and Pink and Coho salmon.
