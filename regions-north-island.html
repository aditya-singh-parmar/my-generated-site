Some of the <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-viewing/">Wildlife</a></em> you will see here includes <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/orca-whales/">Orca Whales</a></em>, <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/humpback-whales/">Humpback Whales</a></em>, <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/pacific-white-sided-dolphins/">Pacific White-sided Dolphins</a></em>, <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/sea-otters/">Sea Otters</a></em>, <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/river-otters/">River Otters</a></em>, <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/steller-sea-lions/"><em>Steller</em> </a>and <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/california-sea-lions/"><em>California</em>sealions</a>, and many species of <em>birds</em>.</p> </div></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083928/vancouver-island/4orcas-paulcottis.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/4Orcas-PaulCottis.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Whale Watching</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p>Northern Resident Orcas, Transient Orcas, and Humpback Whales can be seen on whale watching trips from the North Island. The whale watching here is the most action packed of any place on Vancouver Island.</p> </div></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083980/vancouver-island/blackbeartm.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/BlackBearTM.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Black Bear watching</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p>Spring and early summer see <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/black-bears/">Black bears</a></em> coming out of hibernation. You will probably see bears as you are driving. Because Black bears are such a solid black color, they are hard to miss. Make sure when you stop to give other drivers lots of warning and pull over to the side of the road.</p> </div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-3"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper gdlr-show-all no-skin" style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083980/vancouver-island/grizzlybear-timmelling.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/GrizzlyBear-TimMelling.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Grizzly Bear watching</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><p>There are a few places you can go to see <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/grizzly-bears/">Grizzly Bears</a></em> by boat towards the mainland in the Great Bear Rainforest. Companies will take you along the shore to see Grizzly Bears feeding alone, in groups or along with their cubs. Bears feed on shellfish, salmon, and sedge grasses.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> </div></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083875/vancouver-island/northcoasttrail3-timgage.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/NorthCoastTrail3-TimGage.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Hiking</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item" style="margin-bottom: 50px;"><p>Both the <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/overnight-hikes-by-region/north-island/the-north-coast-trail/"><em>North Coast</em> </a>and <em><a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/overnight-hikes-by-region/north-island/cape-scott-trail/">Cape Scott </a>trail</em>s provide a physical outlet into the most rugged parts of Vancouver Island. Both are overnight trails, though you can do day hikes into the Cape Scott trail.</p> </div></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-image-frame-item gdlr-item"><div class="gdlr-frame frame-type-border"><div class="gdlr-image-link-shortcode"><img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776083981/vancouver-island/nimpkishlake3-bradjohnson-2.jpg" alt="" width="920" height="500"><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/NimpkishLake3-BradJohnson-2.jpg" data-rel="fancybox"><span class="gdlr-image-link-overlay">&nbsp;</span><span class="gdlr-image-link-icon"><i class="fa icon-search"></i></span></a></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item-title-wrapper gdlr-item pos-center "><div class="gdlr-item-title-head"><h3 class="gdlr-item-title gdlr-skin-title gdlr-skin-border">Windsurfing Nimpkish Lake</h3><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item" style="margin-bottom: 50px;"><p>You want to be there on days when it is blowing northwest winds and it will be great if it is fogged out in <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/regions/north-island/port-mcneill/">Port McNeil</a> but clear south of town.&nbsp; This is not a place for beginners as wind gusts can come up quickly taking you by surprise. Make sure you bring lots of bug spray since the bugs will leave you itching for days afterward.
