Located in the same area, Fisgard is Canada’s oldest West Coast lighthouse (1860) and still fully operational, while Fort Rodd was an old gun battery built to protect the navy base at nearby Esquimalt Harbor.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-10"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper " style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#bfbfbf;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#e0e0e0;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#e0e0e0; color: #454545; "><h3>Ogden Point<br> <a href="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/OgdenPoint-AlexRau.jpg"><img class="alignleft wp-image-8622 size-medium" src="https://discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/OgdenPoint-AlexRau-300x169.jpg" alt="OgdenPoint-AlexRau" width="300" height="169"></a></h3> <p>Either you decide to stay in the shallow end of the pool or you go out into the ocean. The famous quote by the late Christopher Reeve can be taken as something more than a metaphor at Ogden Point. At nearly 800 metres long, the point extends into the ocean and offers a unique opportunity to walk out to sea and enjoy great views in all directions. Make sure that you have plenty of time as the return walk takes about 30 minutes. Be very careful as there are no railings on either side, so the walk is only recommended for the adventurous and well prepared.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-11"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper gdlr-show-all no-skin" style="background-color: #f4f4f4; "><div class="container"><div class="six columns"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#55679b;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#a0aed6;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#a0aed6; color: #454545; "><h3>The&nbsp;Robert Bateman Center</h3> <p>&nbsp;– The Robert Bateman Centre&nbsp;is fast becoming one of the finest art galleries, not only in Victoria but Canada as well. Displaying the definitive collection of his life’s work, the Bateman Centre offers many exhibits that expand and espouse on man’s place in nature. Inspirational as well as philosophical, the individual is taken through many commentaries that forces one to come to grips with the dichotomy of our natural world. Come and see why Robert Bateman is considered one of Canada’s most celebrated artists and conservationists. The Robert Bateman Centre is located at 470 Belleville Street, in the Steamship Terminal on Victoria’s Inner Harbor.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="six columns"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#d67e7e;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#f2a7a7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#f2a7a7; color: #454545; "><h3>Government House and Gardens</h3> <p>Government House is the estate of the Lieutenant Governor of British Columbia, the Queen’s royal representative in British Columbia. Government House is a national historic site embracing 36 acres, including rare Garry oak woodlands and ornamental gardens in various styles. Government House is located in the heart of the Rockland neighborhood in Victoria, at 1401 Rockland Avenue, a 5-minute drive or 20-minute walk from downtown. With occasional exceptions, the grounds are open daily free of charge to the public from sunrise to sunset. Visit and enjoy a cup of tea at the interpretative center, Tuesday to Saturday from May through September.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-12"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper " style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="three-fifth columns"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#bfed42;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cfed7d;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cfed7d; color: #454545; "><h3>Hatley Castle</h3> <p>A castle masquerading as a University, or is the other way around? Either way, the estate of the distinguished James Dunsmuir is elegantly crafted. As you walk the grounds, you might start to wonder: Have I seen this place before? Well if you have watched the X-Men film series, you might recognize Hatley Castle as the famed home of Professor Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters. And speaking of schools, the grounds are now home to the Royal Roads University. The university and its gardens are also surrounded by an urban forest…and Wolverine.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="two-fifth columns"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#53c6ef;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#a5dbef;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#a5dbef; color: #454545; "><h3>Emily Carr House</h3> <p>is the childhood home of Emily Carr, a Canadian icon, who’s Modernist and Post-Impressionist paintings helped change the way art was noticed and perceived. Take a walk back to the ambiance her family knew in the 1870s and explore her early sculptures and pottery.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-13"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper " style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="eight columns"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#698c8c;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#90afaf;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#90afaf; color: #454545; "><h3>The Art Gallery of Greater Victoria</h3> <p>&nbsp;houses one of the world’s finest collections of Asian art, with special historic and contemporary exhibits on display throughout the year.
