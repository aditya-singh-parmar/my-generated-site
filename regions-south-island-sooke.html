North Sooke is home to hundreds of kilometers of logging roads that lead through the Rural Resource Lands of the Juan de Fuca electoral area, giving access to several community lakes and small reservoirs. Bear Creek and Diversion reservoirs are also popular destinations in Sooke.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#cecece;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5; color: #828994; "><h4><i class="gdlr-icon fa icon-star" style="color: #04756F; font-size: 30px; "></i>Mountain Biking</h4> <p>Mountain biking&nbsp;is a popular and fast-growing sport British Columbia, and Sooke is quickly establishing itself as a destination with beautiful trails for all skill levels. The&nbsp;popular Galloping Goose Regional Trail (Trans-Canada Trail) leads cyclists, hikers and joggers through Sooke. Many locals enjoy riding the Galloping Goose and spending the afternoon at restaurants along the way.</p> <p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>More challenging rides can be found near the end of Harbourview Road. One of the festivities includes the logger sports show! Sooke’s festivals and events usually take place year round, though peak times are from May to September. Sooke River Music Festival and the Sooke Bluesgrass Festival are some of Sooke’s largest musical festivals.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-normal"><i class="fa fa-check-circle-o" style="color:#1e73be;"></i><br><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4 style="text-align: left;">Salmon Spawning</h4> <p style="text-align: left;">Learn about the importance of salmon runs and how critical the protection of salmon habitat is from the&nbsp;Charters River Interpretive Centre&nbsp;and demonstration hatchery. You can find the salmon interpretive center at 2895 Sooke River Road. Over a dozen rivers and creeks in the Sooke area can benefit greatly from restoring and rehabilitating the salmon habitat.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="four columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-normal"><i class="fa fa-check-circle-o" style="color:#81d742;"></i><br><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4 style="text-align: left;">Art Shows</h4> <p style="text-align: left;">Sooke is home to a popular Vancouver Island art show that features pieces created by countless acclaimed artists. Each year, artists lend the opportunity for onlookers to view their beautiful artwork during the Sooke Studio Tour.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="eight columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-normal"><i class="fa fa-check-circle-o" style="color:#dd3333;"></i><br><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4 style="text-align: left;">Race Rocks Ecological Reserve</h4> <p style="text-align: left;">Blanketing the small islands around Sooke, the plankton density at Race Rocks Ecological Reserve attracts marine mammals looking to feast on the organisms. The waters outside of Vancouver Island allow for a dangerously gripping kayak experience. If you’ve only dabbled in paddling through sheltered sea passages, be prepared for a wild ride. Although the waters can be dangerous, if you carefully plan your trip in the summer months, you can experience the well-behaved side of the Pacific Ocean. The ocean route between Port Renfrew and Sooke can be paddled in the summer, but prepare for a long day. For a less intense experience, you can opt for a partial exploration of the coast in your kayak outing.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#c4c4c4;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5; color: #828994; "><h4>Golfing</h4> <p>Golfers can enjoy the 9-hole John Phillips Memorial Golf Course that’s welcome to the public. Tour all the nearby golf courses such as Olympic View Golf Club, Metchosin Golf Course, Bear Mountain Golf and Country Club, and Royal Colwood Golf Club.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#a0a6f7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>Galloping Goose Trail</h4> <p>The Galloping Goose is a diverse trail that’s fit for virtually all travel activities. The ‘Goose’ includes over 100 km of trails that weave through the communities of Colwood, Metchosin, Langford, Saanich, and Sooke, leading all the way into Victoria. The Galloping Goose can even guide you through the Saanich Peninsula to the Swartz Bay Ferry Terminal (Sidney). Many tourists and locals prefer to explore the rural part of the Goose that runs from Roche Cove Regional Park through to the Sooke Potholes.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#d6d6d6;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5; color: #828994; "><h4>Juan de Fuca Marine Trail&nbsp;</h4> <p>Open since 1995, The&nbsp;Juan de Fuca Marine Trail&nbsp;is often seen as an alternative to the world-renowned West Coast Trail. This trail is considered ambitious, so be prepared for a day of heart pumping, wilderness hiking.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#9ba1f7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>Kludahk Forest Recreation Trails</h4> <p>The trailheads of the&nbsp;Kludahk Forest Recreation Trails&nbsp;are located west end of the Galloping Goose Trail in Sooke, and follows 30 km’s along the San Juan Ridge. The trail is made up of old logging roads through the Jordan Meadows, that’s painted with dense fields of lilies and blueberries. There are five public cabins located along the trail for day-hikers that seek shelter.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#d6d6d6;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#f5f5f5; color: #828994; "><h4>Point no Point Resort</h4> <p>Point no Point is a cabin resort that was established in the 1950’s in French Beach Provincial Park. Point no Point is filled with rich history and astonishing views of orca pods, gray whales, humpback whales, and otters. The resort can be accessed approximately 3.5 km north of French Beach Provincial Park.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-item-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-styled-box-item"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head-wrapper"><div class="gdlr-styled-box-corner" style="border-bottom-color:#9ea4f7;"></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7;"></div></div><div class="gdlr-styled-box-body with-head" style="background-color:#cacdf7; color: #828994; "><h4>China Beach</h4> <p>Take to exploring a hidden waterfall and quick rainforest trails at China Beach Campground, west of Jordan River. You can check out the Sooke Visitor center to learn more.</p> </div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-7"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper " style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><h3 style="text-align: center;">Hiking and Provincial Parks</h3> </div><div class="clear"></div><div class="three-fifth columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-normal"><i class="fa fa-tree" style="color:#8224e3;"></i><br><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4 style="text-align: left;">Sooke Potholes Regional Park</h4> <p style="text-align: left;">Sooke has always been a popular tourist haven, with incredible natural attractions such as Sooke Potholes Regional Park and Whiffin Spit Park. Visitors and locals frequently gather information about Sooke’s history from the Sooke Regional Museum and Visitor Centre.</p> <p style="text-align: left;">The area has greatly increased in popularity, partly because it makes the perfect base to begin tours of the area’s unforgettable wilderness, such as the&nbsp;West Coast Trail&nbsp;and the&nbsp;Juan de Fuca Provincial Park (Juan de Fuca Marine Trail).</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="two-fifth columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-normal"><i class="fa fa-tree" style="color:#1e73be;"></i><br><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4 style="text-align: left;">East Sooke Regional Park</h4> <p style="text-align: left;">Rural east Sooke is home to the 3512-acre East Sooke Regional Park. It is one of the more spectacular parks in the area and attracts those looking for challenging hikes in a wilderness setting. The 10 km (6 mile) Coast Trail takes you through dense forest, along picturesque windswept bluffs, and down to the ocean’s shore.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="twelve columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-normal"><i class="fa fa-tree" style="color:#81d742;"></i><br><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h3 style="text-align: left;">Matheson Lake Regional Park&nbsp;</h3> <p style="text-align: left;">Matheson Lake Regional Park&nbsp;is positioned at the base of Mount Matheson in Metchosin. The park is a sheltered and serine paddling destination that’s perfect for someone who is seeking peaceful wilderness. To get there, go from Hwy 17 and follow through the Rocky Point Road entrance. Launching your hand-carried boat is easy with the short walk from the parking lot to the beach. Take off in your canoe to examine the small islands and creatures that complete the lake.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div><div class="six columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-normal"><i class="fa fa-tree" style="color:#dd3333;"></i><br><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4 style="text-align: left;">Roche Cove Regional Park&nbsp;</h4> <p style="text-align: left;">Neighbouring Matheson Lake Park, Roche Cove Regional Park&nbsp;boasts natural attractions like a remote cove near the Sooke Basin, a selection of calming beaches, and forested trails. Whether in the mood for a peaceful walk or an energetic hike, the selection trails can provide you with relaxation and exercise.</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="six columns"><div class="gdlr-box-with-icon-ux gdlr-ux"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-box-with-icon-item pos-top type-normal"><i class="fa fa-tree" style="color:#25bae8;"></i><br><h4 class="box-with-icon-title"></h4><div class="clear"></div><div class="box-with-icon-caption"><h4 style="text-align: left;">French Beach Provincial Park</h4> <p style="text-align: left;">Families can enjoy the recreational area at French Beach Provincial Park that’s suitable for all ages. The beach, located off Hwy 14, is the perfect place for your family to beachcomb, swim, enjoy a picnic, and admire wildlife. The beach is nicely protected from the oceanic waves from the Olympic Peninsula, and you can sometimes spot gray whales feeding in the water!</p> </div></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-8"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper gdlr-show-all no-skin" style="background-color: #efefef; "><div class="container"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><h3 style="text-align: center;">History &amp; Climate</h3> <p>The name Sooke derives from the T’sou-ke First Nation and it’s quite possible that it represents a stickle-back fish found in Sooke River. The T’sou-ke First Nation has built and maintained a long-lasting legacy that has proven valuable to the community and enriches locals to this day.</p> <p>Some of the mildest weather in western Canada is found in Sooke. The summer months are dry and warm, and the winter months can be wet while remaining quite mild. Visitors from around the globe are eager to witness the natural and unspoiled beauty of the rugged west coast of Vancouver Island.</p> <p>The southwestern end of Sooke is considered to be as sharp as glass, so much so that Point no Point (as mentioned above) can only be seen from certain angles. The shoreline runs from Point no Point, sweeping into Juan de Fuca Point.</p> </div><div class="clear"></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section id="content-section-9"><div class="gdlr-color-wrapper " style="background-color: #ffffff; "><div class="container"><div class="gdlr-item gdlr-content-item"><h3 style="text-align: center;">Additional Info</h3> <p>Jordan River’s logging community has excellent views of the ocean and is located west of Sooke. Take Hwy 14 to Jordan River before it starts to go up the San Juan Ridge.</p> <p>The communities of Metchosin, Langford and Colwood are located to the east of Sooke. These communities are rural and residential areas, considered a part of Greater Victoria. These areas offer many parks, beaches and golf courses to explore.</p> </div><div class="clear"></div><div class="clear"></div></div></div><div class="clear"></div></section><section
