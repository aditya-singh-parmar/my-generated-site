                      Vancouver Island Fast Facts - Learn more about Vancouver Island with DVI

                      <div class="gdlr-blog-content"><p>When people first hear about Vancouver Island, they tend to picture a small, uninhabited area with beaches and not much else. In reality, Vancouver Island is a large, populated Island with incredible diversity, wildlife and things to see, explore and do. Here are some fast facts about the&nbsp;Island and its different areas.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <div id="attachment_12420" style="width: 785px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12420" class="size-full wp-image-12420" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776084200/vancouver-island/susan-johnston-e1508187925610.jpg" alt="Photo by: Susan Johnston" width="775" height="321" srcset="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Susan-Johnston-e1508187925610.jpg 775w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Susan-Johnston-e1508187925610-300x124.jpg 300w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Susan-Johnston-e1508187925610-768x318.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 775px) 100vw, 775px"><p id="caption-attachment-12420" class="wp-caption-text">Photo by: Susan Johnston</p></div> <p>Size: Vancouver Island is about 460km long and 100km wide, with around 32,134km2 total area. It is the eleventh largest Island in Canada.</p> <p>Population: As of 2011, just under 760,000 people lived on the Island.</p> <p>Number of Cities: About fifty towns and cities are on Vancouver Island.</p> <p>Biggest City: <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/south-island/victoria/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Victoria</a>, British Columbia’s capital city, is the Island’s largest city with a population of around 84,000.</p> <p>Smallest City: Zeballos is one of the Island’s smallest towns, with a population of 110.</p> <p>Regions: Vancouver Island is typically split into six regions – <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/north-island/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">North Island</a>, <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/central-island/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Central Island</a>, <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/south-island/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">South Island</a>, the <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/gulf-islands/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Gulf Islands</a>, <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/cowichan-region/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cowichan</a> and the <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/pacific-rim/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pacific Rim</a>.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <div id="attachment_12414" style="width: 785px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12414" class="size-full wp-image-12414" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776084200/vancouver-island/david-wadsworth-e1508187488261.jpg" alt="Photo by: David Wadsworth" width="775" height="321" srcset="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/David-Wadsworth-e1508187488261.jpg 775w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/David-Wadsworth-e1508187488261-300x124.jpg 300w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/David-Wadsworth-e1508187488261-768x318.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 775px) 100vw, 775px"><p id="caption-attachment-12414" class="wp-caption-text">Photo by: David Wadsworth</p></div> <p>First Nations Groups: There are three main First Nations groups on Vancouver Island. The Kwakwaka’wakw, Nuu-chah-nulth and Coast Salish. Within the band are various other groups with unique cultures and governments.</p> <p>Famous People: Vancouver Island was called home by plenty of notable names including Pamela Anderson, Randy Bachman, Kim Catrall and Nelly Furtado.</p> <p>World Records: The Island holds plenty of world titles, including the world’s largest hockey stick in <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/cowichan-region/duncan/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Duncan</a>, and a Guinness Record for the largest street hockey tournament, which took place in <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/cowichan-region/ladysmith/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ladysmith</a>. It also has the world’s largest totem pole in Victoria, B.C.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <div id="attachment_12417" style="width: 785px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12417" class="size-full wp-image-12417" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776084200/vancouver-island/istvan-hernadi-e1508187752916.jpg" alt="Photo by: Istvan Hernadi" width="775" height="321" srcset="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Istvan-Hernadi-e1508187752916.jpg 775w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Istvan-Hernadi-e1508187752916-300x124.jpg 300w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Istvan-Hernadi-e1508187752916-768x318.jpg 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 775px) 100vw, 775px"><p id="caption-attachment-12417" class="wp-caption-text">Photo by: Istvan Hernadi</p></div> <p>Km of Coastline: Vancouver Island has about 3400km of coastline.</p> <p>Km of Mountain Range: The Vancouver Island Mountain Range runs the length of the entire Island. They have an area of 45,373km2, with the highest point being the summit of the Golden Hinde at 2,195m. The mountains make for some great <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/top-hiking-spots/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">hiking</a>.</p> <p>Animal Unique to Island: The Vancouver Island Marmot is unique to its namesake and is distinct from other marmot types. They are considered an endangered species, and large conservation programs are working to re-establish their population.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <div id="attachment_12416" style="width: 785px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12416" class="size-full wp-image-12416" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776084201/vancouver-island/harrison-stoker-e1508187683425.jpg" alt="Photo by: Harrison Stoker" width="775" height="321" srcset="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Harrison-Stoker-e1508187683425.jpg 775w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Harrison-Stoker-e1508187683425-300x124.jpg 300w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Harrison-Stoker-e1508187683425-768x318.jpg 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 775px) 100vw, 775px"><p id="caption-attachment-12416" class="wp-caption-text">Photo by: Harrison Stoker</p></div> <p># <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/vineyards-vancouver-island/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Wineries</a>: From the South Island, through the Cowichan Valley and to <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/north-central/courtenay-comox/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Comox Valley</a>, there are around&nbsp;37 licensed wineries.</p> <p># <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/farmers-markets-products-vancouver-island/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Farmer’s Markets</a>: More than 40 Farmer’s Markets take place on the Island. The Duncan Farmer’s Market is considered one of the top markets in British Columbia.</p> <p>Diving: Vancouver Island has some of the best cold-water diving in the world. Nootka Sound and Hornby Island are some of the few places in the world to see six-gilled sharks.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <div id="attachment_12421" style="width: 785px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12421" class="size-full wp-image-12421" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776084201/vancouver-island/tom-hazen-e1508187975536.jpg" alt="Photo by: Tom Hazen" width="775" height="321" srcset="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Tom-Hazen-e1508187975536.jpg 775w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Tom-Hazen-e1508187975536-300x124.jpg 300w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Tom-Hazen-e1508187975536-768x318.jpg 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 775px) 100vw, 775px"><p id="caption-attachment-12421" class="wp-caption-text">Photo by: Tom Hazen</p></div> <p>High and Low Temperature: The Island has an average coldest temperature of -2 degrees Celsius in the Winter and an average high of 23 degrees Celsius in the summer. It is considered to have the mildest climate in Canada.</p> <p>Amount of Rain/Year: Vancouver Island sees over 20,000mm of rain per year, with <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/south-island/port-renfrew/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Port Renfrew</a> and <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/pacific-rim/tofino/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Tofino</a> seeing the most. Victoria, thanks to the rain shadow of the mountains and the mainland, sees the least.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <div id="attachment_12413" style="width: 785px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12413" class="size-full wp-image-12413" src="https://res.cloudinary.com/djqswlfat/image/upload/v1776084202/vancouver-island/barbara-evans-e1508187401182.jpg" alt="Photo by: Barbara Evans" width="775" height="322" srcset="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barbara-Evans-e1508187401182.jpg 775w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barbara-Evans-e1508187401182-300x125.jpg 300w, https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/Barbara-Evans-e1508187401182-768x319.jpg 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 775px) 100vw, 775px"><p id="caption-attachment-12413" class="wp-caption-text">Photo by: Barbara Evans</p></div> <p>Natural Features: Della Falls, close to <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/regions/north-central/campbell-river/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Campbell River</a>, is Canada’s highest <a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/blog/waterfalls/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">waterfall</a>, and ranks top ten in the world. It is 440m high.</p> <p>Tall Trees: The Island has some of the tallest and oldest spruce trees and cedars in the world, with some over 310ft tall, and over 1000 years old.</p> <p><a href="https://www.discovervancouverisland.com/wildlife-on-vancouver-island/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Animals</a>: Vancouver Island is thought to have the densest population of black bears, with a population of about 7,000. It’s home to over 7,000 different water species, and over 200 species of migratory birds.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <p>Contributed by: Laurissa Cebryk</p> </div>
